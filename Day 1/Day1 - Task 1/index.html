Tribute to A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

About A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, the "Missile Man of India", was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.
He was an aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India.
His contributions to India's missile and space programs were monumental.
A deeply humble man, he inspired millions with his vision and work ethic.
He remains a symbol of perseverance, innovation, and patriotism.

Achievements

  • Developed India's first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III)
  • Led India's first successful nuclear test in 1998
  • Wrote books like "Wings of Fire" and "India 2020"
  • Received the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award
